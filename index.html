What is Requirements?

Requirements

is a singular documented physical or functional need that a particular design, product or process aims to satisfy.

According to the Guide to the Business Analysis Body of Knowledge

    Requirements are:
  • A condition or capability needed by a stakeholder to solve a problem or achieve an objective

According to the Guide to the Business Analysis Body of Knowledge

    Requirements are
  • A condition or capability that must be met or possessed by a solution or solution component to satisfy a contract, standard, specification, or other formally imposed documents.

According to the Guide to the Business Analysis Body of Knowledge

    Requirements are
  • A documented representation of a condition or capability as in (1) or (2).

Engineering

Requirements Engineering

is the process of defining, documenting, and maintaining requirements in the engineering design process.

Requirements Engineering

4 Major Phases of Requirements Engineering

1. Requirements Elicitation

1. Requirements Elicitation

⇾ Is the practice of researching and discovering the requirements of a system from users, customers, and other stakeholders.

2. Requirements Analysis

2. Requirements Analysis

⇾ Focuses on the tasks that determine the needs or conditions to meet the new or altered product or project, taking account of the possibly conflicting requirements of the various stakeholders, analyzing, documenting, validating and managing software or system requirements.

3. Requirements Documentation

3. Requirements Documentation

⇾ In project management describes how each requirement meets the business needs for the project. Requirements should be measurable, traceable, consistent, complete and acceptable to the stakeholders.

4. Requirements Verification & Validation

4. Requirements Verification & Validation

⇾ Are independent procedures that are used together for checking that a product, service, or system meets requirements and specifications and that it fulfills its intended purpose.
